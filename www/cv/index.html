EDUCATION
  • 2018 PhD, Computer Music and Multimedia, Brown University, Providence (expected May 27)

    Dissertation: Toward a Minor Data Science: Rhythms with the Earth, the Animal, and the Machine

    This dissertation presents an artistic methodology derived from Henri Lefebvre’s Rhythmanalysis and its application to media ecologies of more-than-human agency. Each of three artworks addresses a contentious relationship between humans and a form of nonhuman: Animas with a river degraded by extractive industries, Urban Intonation with the ubiquitous brown rat with which we cohabitate in expanding urban environments, and Everything That Happens Will Happen Today with emerging artificial intelligence algorithms embedded in consumer systems. These pieces situate data not as knowledge, but as transductive media that bring different rhythmic bodies into affective contact. They are case studies of a “minor data science” that resists the ontologies of platform capitalism.

    Committee: Wendy Chun (co-advisor), Ed Osborn, Rebecca Schneider, Todd Winkler (co-advisor)
